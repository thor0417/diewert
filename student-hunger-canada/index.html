Why existing solutions fall short

Food Banks Weren't Built for Students

Current solutions — food banks, hampers, campus pantries — carry social stigma and offer limited food choice. Many students who need help never seek it because they fear being seen.

A student skipping meals to pay rent deserves more than a pre-packed box and a line-up.

  • Students avoid food banks due to fear of stigma and being identified
  • Pre-packed hampers ignore dietary needs, allergies, and cultural preferences
  • Campus pantries have limited hours, limited stock, and high visibility
  • Shame and stigma cause many students to suffer in silence rather than seek help

How we help

A Better Way to Feed Students

Transparent, dignified, and built to scale across Canada.

Step 1

Donors and merchants contribute

Individual donors give online. Merchants contribute $1.50 per eligible transaction through the Cocendo program.

Step 2

Funds are processed securely

The Foundation processes and allocates funds with full CRA compliance. 90% goes directly to student grocery support.

Step 3

Students receive a digital grocery card

Private, trackable digital cards are issued directly to students — no application line-up, no public sign-up.

Step 4

Students shop with dignity

Cards are redeemable at major Canadian retailers. Students choose their own groceries — no stigma, no hampers, no labels.
