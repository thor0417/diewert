Our Approach

Grocery support, chosen by students

We don't run a food bank. We don't hand out hampers. Post-secondary students access digital grocery cards and choose their own food -- the way anyone else shops. No line-ups, no judgment, no stigma.

This model is unique in Canada, made possible in part by the Cocendo Social Engine -- a platform that turns everyday merchant transactions into student meal funding. When partnered merchants process eligible transactions, $1.00 goes directly to the Foundation.

The Issue

Food insecurity affects learning, not just health

When students don't have reliable access to food, the effects reach well beyond nutrition. Hunger is one of the leading but least-discussed barriers to post-secondary completion in Canada.

  • Rising costs, fixed budgets

    Tuition, rent, and transportation leave little room for groceries. First-generation students and international students are disproportionately affected and least likely to ask for help.

  • Concentration and performance

    Students who are food insecure report lower grades, reduced ability to concentrate in class, and higher rates of academic withdrawal. Addressing food access directly improves outcomes.

  • Dignity matters

    Traditional food bank models carry stigma that prevents many students from seeking help. Digital grocery cards let students shop like everyone else -- with privacy and full choice over what they eat.
